Research project

Consensia — Multi‑role AI Consensus for Software Engineering

Can a large language model be a trustworthy judge? Consensia explores stakeholder‑aware, evidence‑backed consensus across software engineering tasks — code, diagrams, and architectures — by orchestrating multiple expert personas and a meta‑evaluator.

Project Aim

We investigate whether an LLM can issue trustworthy, explainable judgments by comparing and scoring multi-agent outputs. Consensia produces a final verdict with rationale, grounded in tool evidence (tests, linters, audits) rather than text alone.

This work is inspired by our course research on “LLM as a Judge” led by our advisor Prof. Anıl Koyuncu at Bilkent University.

How it works

Consensia coordinates multiple expert roles to generate artifacts and critiques, then tasks a meta‑judge to score candidates under a rubric. The verdict references specific evidence (e.g., tests passed, linter scores, threat findings).

Expert Roles

Each role applies a tailored rubric — correctness, scalability, reliability, cost, and clarity — to form a diverse consensus.

Project Status

MVP in development with Developer, Architect, and Security roles active. Upcoming: QA, SRE, and visualization dashboard.
